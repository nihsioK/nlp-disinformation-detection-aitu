In English:
A Hybrid Approach to Disinformation Detection in Political Statements: Combining Textual Features and Speaker Metadata

2. Черновик аннотации

The spread of disinformation in social networks and digital political communication makes automatic credibility assessment an important research problem. However, many existing approaches still rely mainly on textual signals and are less effective when the task requires fine-grained truthfulness classification rather than simple binary fake news detection. This study investigates NLP methods for detecting disinformation in political statements using the LIAR dataset, which provides six truthfulness classes together with speaker-related metadata and historical credibility information. The first stage of the work includes a reproducible preprocessing and evaluation pipeline, TF-IDF based baseline models, and a RoBERTa-based text classification experiment. The best baseline result was achieved by SVM with macro-F1 of 0.2533, while RoBERTa improved the score to 0.2758 on the validation set. Although the transformer model showed a positive effect, the improvement remained limited, which indicates that text-only modeling is not sufficient for this task. The next stage of the research is therefore focused on a hybrid content + context architecture that combines statement semantics with speaker metadata and credibility history. The expected contribution of the study is a more effective and better motivated framework for multi-class disinformation detection in political text.
